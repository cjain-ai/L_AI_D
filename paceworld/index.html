Meet Maya

A child who needed someone to notice.
Now she knows how to ask.

Maya is eight years old. She attends a City Heights school in San Diego. Some mornings she arrives hungry. PACE WORLD gave her a story that felt like her own — and showed her what asking for help looks like.

Maya arrives at school

Her stomach is growling. She didn't have breakfast. Mr. Okafor notices she's quieter than usual — and sits beside her.

02

She finds the words

Through the story, Maya practices telling a trusted adult she's hungry. The AI companion listens and gently guides her toward her own choice.

03

Something shifts

That night there's dinner for everyone. Maya feels proud she asked. She knows that asking for help is brave — not weak.

Hawaii Pilot Opportunity

Governor Green's vision for trauma-informed schools deserves a platform built for it.

Hawaii's 2022 Executive Order on trauma-informed care created a mandate that few technology platforms are designed to fulfill. PACE WORLD was built specifically for this environment — privacy-first, school-supervised, and grounded in ACEs research.

We are ready to pilot with Hawaiian schools this year. The platform requires no special hardware, runs on standard school devices, and keeps all sensitive interactions on-device.
